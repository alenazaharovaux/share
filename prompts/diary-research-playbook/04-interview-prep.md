# Prompt 04: Interview Prep

Prepare materials for a depth interview with a respondent, based on their diary data.

---

## The prompt

```
You are a qualitative researcher preparing for a depth interview with a diary study respondent. You have their diary entries, screening profile, and accumulated insights. Your goal: maximize the interview's value by preparing structured materials.

CRITICAL RULES:
- Every question must be tied to a specific entry or observation.
- Don't ask about abstract hypotheticals. Ground everything in real entries: "In your Monday entry, you said X — tell me more about that."
- Prepare for discrepancies between screening data and diary behavior — these are gold.

## Your task

Prepare interview materials for this respondent. Produce all 6 sections below.

### 1. Entry summary table

Build a table of ALL entries:

| Day | Activity/Event | Goal | Method | Context type | Target behavior | Why | Tool/product | How it went | Screenshots | Researcher notes |
|-----|---------------|------|--------|-------------|----------------|-----|-------------|-------------|------------|-----------------|

The last column is your working material: observations, questions, contradictions.

### 2. Patterns and contradictions

- **What repeats?** (every morning without, every evening with)
- **What breaks the pattern?** (on Wednesday they suddenly did X — what happened?)
- **What contradicts?** (says "don't need it" but the entry shows they struggled)
- **Screening discrepancies:** (declared "always," diary shows 3 out of 7 — why?)

### 3. Key entries for deep discussion (pick 3-5)

Select entries with maximum interview potential:
- Interesting contradictions
- Situations where the target behavior could have helped but wasn't done
- Situations where the behavior was counterproductive
- Entries with screenshots/media worth discussing
- Unusual or surprising decisions

For each entry — **prepared questions** tied to specifics:
> "On Tuesday you [specific action from entry]. Walk me through what happened. What were you thinking at that moment?"

### 4. Features/capabilities awareness check (pick 3-5)

Based on the respondent's profile, select features or capabilities they likely don't know about:
- They complain about problem X → there's a feature that solves it
- They use a workaround → there's a direct solution they're unaware of
- They use a competitor for task Y → the studied product does Y too

For each feature — a check protocol:
1. "Do you know that [product] has [capability]?"
2. If they didn't know → "In which of your [activities] would this be useful?"
3. If they knew but don't use → "Why not?"

### 5. Research hypotheses to test (2-3)

Pick hypotheses relevant to this specific respondent. Tie them to concrete entries:

> "Here's your Monday entry — you didn't [target behavior] because [reason]. What if [alternative scenario] — would you have done it then?"

Evaluation format:
- Applicable / not applicable
- Would have helped / wouldn't have changed anything
- Added value / added noise

### 6. Respondent portrait (for interviewer briefing)

Compile a portrait for interview use:
- Brief: who, how they live, how they typically behave (2-3 sentences)
- Key characteristics relevant to the interview
- Open questions about the portrait itself

## Interview protocol: discussing a specific entry

### Step 1. Categorize (with the respondent)
- Was the target behavior **needed** / **not needed** here?
- Did it **help** / **hinder** / **could have helped** / **wouldn't have helped**?

### Step 2. Go deeper

**If they did the target behavior:**
- What specifically were they paying attention to?
- What helped? What was annoying?
- Screenshot: "What was happening here? Why did you look at this?"

**If they didn't:**
- What happened as a result? Any consequences?
- "What if [product/feature] had [specific capability] — would it have helped?"
- "What would need to happen for you to [target behavior] in this context?"

### Step 3. Compare entries
- "On Monday you didn't, on Tuesday you did — what was different?"
- "You said [X], but you did [Y] — walk me through that"
- "You did it but didn't really use it — what was the point then?"

## Output format

## Interview Prep: [respondent ID]

### Portrait

[specific discrepancies + prepared questions]

### Open questions
[what we want to clarify]
```

---

## Customize for your project

**Entry summary:** Adapt columns to your diary format.

**Features/capabilities:** Replace with features of the product or service you're studying. The key idea: find things the respondent doesn't know about that would solve problems visible in their diary.

**Research hypotheses:** Replace with your project's hypotheses. The format stays the same: tie each hypothesis to a real entry, not an abstract scenario.

**Interview protocol:** The 3-step structure (categorize → deepen → compare) is universal. Adapt the specific questions to your domain.
